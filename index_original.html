About Me

Aashish Vivekanand, an Android Security Expert and National Technical Committee Member at the National Cyber Defence Research Centre (NCDRC), is a distinguished Computer Science engineer from Anna University, Chennai. He has devoted considerable time to numerous projects, contributing to open-source initiatives in Android, Internet of Things (IoT), and Cyber-security.

Google, in partnership with Udacity, recognized his potential and awarded him scholarships for the Android Developer Nanodegree program and the Front-End Web Developer program. Upon completion of these programs, he was selected for the pioneering Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree program in collaboration with Mercedes-Benz.

Aashish's innovative work has earned him multiple accolades. He won first prize in the Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) Connect Hackathon in 2015 and 2016, the Dr. Abdul Kalam Young Achiever Award from the World Youth Federation, and the Daimler Mobile App Garage competition. He also represented India at the CAMME 2017 event in Bangkok, where he presented his ideas on "Inventory Transparency For Agricultural Produce Through IoT."

Aashish developed the Android application "Smart Learning" in response to widespread online content piracy. This app halts the playback of streaming content if it detects intrusion from services or other applications. His innovative solution won first place at the all-India Cyber-Security Hackathon, organized by the US Consulate Chennai and Learning Links in 2016.

In addition, Aashish presented a DNS Tweaks program designed to enhance child safety online during the launch of the "Online Child Safety and Security Report India" by UNICEF. His idea was highly appreciated and shared by the Chief UNICEF Representative for India. Aashish also mentored student teams in "The State of World's Children 2017" by UNICEF, raising awareness about various cybersecurity topics among school students.
